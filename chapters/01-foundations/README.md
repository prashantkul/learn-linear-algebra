> 🎧 **Pre-built NotebookLM:** [open it here](https://notebooklm.google.com/notebook/d52edb24-e2e2-43b6-840d-a2c5d7a47519) — audio overview (podcast), quiz, mind map, slide deck, infographic, and chat. Click *"Make a copy"* in NotebookLM to clone it to your own account.

## Learning objectives

By the end of this chapter you will:

- Speak the language of sets and functions confidently (domain, codomain, image, injective, surjective, inverse, composition).
- Understand what makes a function *linear*, and why linearity is the central property the rest of the course studies.
- Visualize ℝ¹, ℝ², ℝ³, and reason about ℝⁿ for n > 3 even though you can't picture it.
- Connect a "vector" as both an arrow in space and an n-tuple of numbers.

## Prerequisites

- High-school algebra (solving linear equations, the xy-plane).
- Comfortable with function notation f(x) = mx + b.
- No calculus required.

## Estimated time

6–8 hours of focused reading, plus exercises.
