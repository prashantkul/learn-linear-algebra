# Chapter 1 — Sources

## Bretscher, *Linear Algebra with Applications* (5e)

- **Appendix A: Vectors** — pp. 457–466. The cleanest treatment of ℝⁿ as n-tuples and as arrows.

## Saveliev, *Linear Algebra Illustrated*

- **Chapter 1: Sets and functions** — pp. 7–87. Ground truth for set notation, the xy-plane, functions, and the precise meaning of "linear."
  - §1.1 Introduction to linear algebra (pp. 7–11) — the coffee-blend motivating example.
  - §1.2–§1.4 Sets, the real number line, set building (pp. 12–24).
  - §1.5–§1.7 Relations, the xy-plane, functions (pp. 25–40).
  - §1.9 Linear relations and functions (pp. 50–56).
  - §1.11 Linear functions (pp. 65–73).
- **Chapter 2: Functions as transformations** — pp. 88–156. Image, one-to-one and onto, composition, inverse.
- **Chapter 4: Multidimensional spaces** — pp. 239–281 (sections 4.1–4.5). Where ℝⁿ "comes from" and how to think about vectors before doing arithmetic with them.
  - Especially §4.4 *Where vectors come from* (pp. 264–273) — the philosophical heart of the chapter.
